Fact / Definition Type Questions

Questions 1 – 50

1 The locus of all particles in a medium, vibrating in the same phase is called ▼
✓ Answer: (c)A wavefront is the locus of all particles vibrating in the same phase. Light is an electromagnetic wave (4000 Å – 7800 Å).
2 Which of the following is correct for light diverging from a point source? ▼
✓ Answer: (a)For a point source, intensity ∝ 1/r². The wavefront is spherical, not parabolic.
3 Wavefront is the locus of all points, where the particles of the medium vibrate with the same ▼
✓ Answer: (a)Phase — by definition.
4 The phenomenon which is NOT explained by Huygens' construction of wavefront ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Huygens' principle does not explain the origin of spectra.
5 Huygens' concept of secondary wave ▼
✓ Answer: (b)Huygens' principle gives a geometrical method of tracing a wavefront.
6 A plane wave passes through a convex lens. The geometrical shape of the wavefront that emerges is ▼
✓ Answer: (c)A convex lens converges plane waves into a converging spherical wavefront.
7 Spherical wavefronts emanating from a point source strike a plane reflecting surface. What happens immediately after reflection? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)After reflection from a plane mirror, the diverging spherical wavefront becomes converging — same magnitude, reversed sign.
8 When light suffers reflection at the interface between water and glass, the change of phase in the reflected wave is ▼
✓ Answer: (b)Phase reversal (π) occurs on reflection because glass is denser than water.
9 Two plane wavefronts incident on (i) a thin convex lens and (ii) a refracting face of a thin prism. The emerging wavefronts are respectively ▼
✓ Answer: (c)Convex lens → spherical (converging); Prism → tilted plane wavefront.
10 When a film is illuminated by white light, its upper portion appears dark. Path difference between two reflected beams at that spot must be ▼
✓ Answer: (b)Dark spot ⟹ destructive interference. Path difference = λ/2 (plus phase change π on reflection gives effective λ → darkness).
11 If two coherent sources are vibrating in phase, we have constructive interference at point P whenever the path difference is ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Constructive interference: path difference = nλ (n = 0, 1, 2, …). Resultant intensity = 4I₀.
12 If two sources are coherent, then the phase difference (φ) between the waves produced by them at any point ▼
✓ Answer: (c)Coherent sources maintain a constant phase difference, so positions of maxima and minima are fixed (stable).
13 The device which produces highly coherent sources is ▼
✓ Answer: (c)Laser emits coordinated waves of exactly the same wavelength — highest coherence.
14 Which of the following CANNOT produce two coherent sources? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)A simple prism cannot produce coherent sources.
15 Coherence is a measure of ▼
✓ Answer: (a)Coherence measures the capability of a wave to produce sustained interference.
16 Two sources of light are said to be coherent when they give light waves of same ▼
✓ Answer: (b)Same wavelength (frequency) with a constant phase difference.
17 Intensity of light depends on ▼
✓ Answer: (a)By wave theory, Intensity ∝ (Amplitude)².
18 The colour of bright fringe nearest to central achromatic fringe in the interference pattern with white light will be ▼
✓ Answer: (a)Fringe width β = λD/d ∝ λ. Since λ_violet is smallest, violet fringe is closest to centre.
19 The correct formula for fringe visibility is ▼
✓ Answer: (a)V = (I_max − I_min) / (I_max + I_min).
20 Laser light is considered to be coherent because it consists of ▼
✓ Answer: (c)Laser is monochromatic and all photons are in phase (stimulated emission).
21 Interfering fringes formed by a thin oil film on water are seen in yellow light of sodium lamp. The fringes are ▼
✓ Answer: (a)Bright fringes are yellow, dark fringes are black (monochromatic illumination).
22 In Young's Double Slit experiment, if the distance between slit and screen (D) is comparable with fringe width (B), the fringe pattern will be ▼
✓ Answer: (b)With the slit separation constant, the locus of point P in the xy-plane is a hyperbola.
23 If Young's double slit experiment is performed in water keeping the rest of the set-up same, the fringes will ▼
✓ Answer: (b)λ_water = λ_air / μ. Since λ decreases and β ∝ λ, fringe width decreases.
24 In YDSE, when we place a converging lens after the slits and the screen at focus of lens, it ▼
✓ Answer: (b)A converging lens introduces no extra path difference; it gives a more intense pattern.
25 The fringe width for red colour compared to violet colour is approximately ▼
✓ Answer: (b)λ_red ≈ 8000 Å, λ_violet ≈ 4000 Å. Since β ∝ λ, β_red ≈ 2 × β_violet.
26 In Young's double slit experiment, the minimum amplitude is obtained when the phase difference of superimposing waves is (n = 1, 2, 3, …) ▼
✓ Answer: (b)For minima, phase difference = odd multiple of π = (2n − 1)π.
27 The fringe width in a Young's double slit experiment can be increased if we DECREASE ▼
✓ Answer: (b)β = λD/d. Decreasing slit separation d increases fringe width.
28 In YDSE, one slit is covered with red filter and another with green filter. The interference pattern will be ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Red and green are complementary colours; they cannot interfere. Pattern is invisible.
29 Instead of two slits, if we use two separate identical sodium lamps in Young's experiment, which occurs? ▼
✓ Answer: (a)Independent sources lack constant phase difference → no stable interference → general illumination.
30 Which of the following is NOT essential for two sources to produce sustained interference in YDSE? ▼
✓ Answer: (b)Equal intensity is not essential; unequal intensities still produce interference (though with reduced contrast).
31 In YDSE, the locus of point P lying in a plane with constant path difference between two interfering waves is ▼
✓ Answer: (a)For two point sources, the locus of points with constant path difference is a hyperbola.
32 If the width of slit in single slit diffraction is doubled, the central maximum becomes ▼
✓ Answer: (b)Width = 2λD/a. Doubling 'a' halves width (sharper). Same energy in smaller area → brighter.
33 A diffraction pattern is obtained using red light. What happens if red is replaced by blue light? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)λ_blue < λ_red, so diffraction bands become narrower and more closely spaced.
34 When monochromatic light is replaced by white light in Fresnel's biprism arrangement, the central fringe is ▼
✓ Answer: (b)At the centre all colours meet in phase → white central fringe.
35 For Fraunhofer diffraction from a single slit, the light wavefront incident should be ▼
✓ Answer: (c)Fraunhofer diffraction requires plane wavefronts (both source and screen effectively at infinity).
36 Diffraction can be treated as interference if the number of coherent sources is ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Single-slit diffraction = interference from infinite coherent secondary sources in the slit.
37 Diffraction effects in a microscopic specimen become important when the separation between two points is ▼
✓ Answer: (c)Diffraction is significant when separation ~ wavelength of light used.
38 What is the Brewster angle for air-to-glass transition? (ₐμg = 1.5) ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Brewster's law: tan iB = μ = 1.5 → iB = tan⁻¹(1.5).
39 When ordinary light is made incident on a quarter wave plate, the emergent light is ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Ordinary (unpolarised) light through a quarter-wave plate emerges as elliptically polarised.
40 Transverse nature of light was confirmed by the phenomenon of ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Only transverse waves can be polarised — polarisation confirmed light is transverse.
41 In linearly polarised light, the magnitude of the electric field vector ▼
✓ Answer: (d)The electric field oscillates (varies periodically) but always in the same plane.
42 Unpolarized light on a plane glass — for reflected and refracted rays to be perpendicular, the angle of incidence satisfies ▼
✓ Answer: (b)Brewster's law: μ = tan iB.
43 Light waves can be polarised because they ▼
✓ Answer: (c)Polarisation is possible only for transverse waves.
44 Light transmitted by a Nicol prism is ▼
✓ Answer: (b)A Nicol prism uses double refraction to transmit only the ordinary ray — plane polarised light.
45 Optically active substances are those which ▼
✓ Answer: (c)Optically active substances (e.g. sugar solution) rotate the plane of polarisation.
46 Polaroid glass is used in sunglasses because ▼
✓ Answer: (a)Polaroid reduces glare by transmitting only one plane of polarisation, halving intensity.
47 In propagation of light waves, the angle between plane of vibration and plane of polarisation is ▼
✓ Answer: (b)90° — plane of vibration and plane of polarisation are mutually perpendicular.
48 In propagation of EM waves, angle between direction of propagation and plane of polarisation is ▼
✓ Answer: (a)Plane of vibration ⊥ direction of propagation, and ⊥ plane of polarisation. So angle between direction of propagation and plane of polarisation = 0°.
49 When unpolarised light is incident on a plane glass at Brewster's angle, which statement is correct? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)At Brewster's angle, reflected light is fully plane polarised; transmitted light is only partially polarised.
50 From Brewster's law of polarisation, the angle of polarisation depends upon ▼
✓ Answer: (a)Refractive index μ depends on wavelength, hence Brewster angle tan⁻¹(μ) depends on wavelength.

Statement Type Questions

Questions 51 – 66

51 Which of the following statements about wavefront shapes are correct? I. Light diverging from a point source → spherical. II. Light from convex lens (point source at focus) → plane. III. Wavefront of light from a distant star intercepted by Earth → plane. ▼
✓ Answer: (d)All three statements are correct: point source → spherical; lens with point source at focus → plane; distant star → plane (portion intercepted by Earth).
52 Which statements are correct? I. A point source emits waves uniformly in all directions. II. In spherical wave, locus of same amplitude and phase are spheres. III. At small distance from source, small portion of sphere ≈ plane wave. ▼
✓ Answer: (b)I and II are correct. III is wrong — at LARGE distance a small portion ≈ plane wave (not small distance).
53 Wavefront passing through a prism: I. Lower portion (B') is delayed → tilt. II. Time for A→A' = Time for B→B'. III. Speed of wavefront is same everywhere. IV. Particle on A'B' in phase with particle on AB. ▼
✓ Answer: (a)I and II: lower portion delayed (denser medium) → tilt; time A→A' = B→B' by Huygens' principle.
54 Which of the following CANNOT be explained on the basis of wave nature of light? I. Polarization   II. Total internal reflection   III. Photoelectric effect   IV. Compton effect ▼
✓ Answer: (b)Photoelectric effect and Compton effect require quantum (particle) nature of light — not explained by wave theory.
55 Conditions for sustained interference: I. Phase difference constant with time. II. Nearly same amplitude levels. III. Same frequency. IV. Waves moving in opposite directions. ▼
✓ Answer: (a)I, II, III. Waves moving in opposite directions (IV) form standing waves, not required for interference pattern.
56 True statements about double-slit interference pattern: I. Equal number of bright and dark band widths. II. Pattern obtained by superposition of two waves from slits. III. Minima at angle λ/a (a = slit separation). ▼
✓ Answer: (c)I and II. Statement III refers to single-slit diffraction minima, not double-slit interference.
57 In double-slit experiment, the pattern on screen is a superposition of __ and __. I. Single-slit diffraction from each slit. II. Double-slit interference pattern. III. Double-slit diffraction. IV. Single-slit interference. ▼
✓ Answer: (a)The pattern = single-slit diffraction envelope (I) × double-slit interference fringes (II).
58 True statements about the single-slit diffraction pattern: I. Central bright maxima has twice the width of others. II. First null at angle λ/2a. III. Intensity of maxima falls as we move away. IV. Bands are of decreasing width. ▼
✓ Answer: (d)I (central max is twice as wide) and III (intensity decreases) are correct. First null is at λ/a (not λ/2a).
59 Diffraction is exhibited by: I. Matter waves   II. Water waves   III. Sound waves   IV. Light waves ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Diffraction is a universal wave phenomenon — all wave types exhibit it.
60 We can hear sound around a wall but not see around it because: I. λ_light > λ_sound. II. λ_light < λ_sound. III. λ_light ≪ dimensions of most obstacles. IV. λ_sound > dimensions of most obstacles. ▼
✓ Answer: (c)λ_light ≪ obstacle size (no diffraction seen); λ_sound ~ obstacle size (diffraction occurs, so we hear around corners). Statements II, III, IV are correct.
61 Necessary and sufficient conditions for good interference and diffraction: I. Slit separation very small (~mm). II. Slit width very small (~mm). III. Screen distance large (~cm). IV. Screen distance small (~mm). ▼
✓ Answer: (c)From β = λD/d: small slit separation (I), small slit width (II), and large screen distance (III) are required.
62 Resolving power of a telescope is limited by: I. Focal length of objective. II. Diameter of objective. III. Wavelength of light. IV. Thickness of objective. ▼
✓ Answer: (c)Resolving power ∝ a/λ (Rayleigh criterion Δθ = 0.61λ/a). Depends on aperture 'a' (II) and wavelength (III).
63 The magnification by the objective lens of a microscope does NOT depend upon ▼
✓ Answer: (d)m = v/f = D/f = 2 tan β. The eyepiece angle does not affect objective magnification.
64 Single-slit Fraunhofer diffraction figure shows: I. Wavelength distribution. II. Intensity distribution. III. Diffraction. IV. Fringe pattern. ▼
✓ Answer: (c)The figure shows intensity distribution (II) and fringe/diffraction pattern (IV).
65 Correct statements for diffraction: I. λ must be comparable to slit size. II. Diffraction is common in sound, not in light. III. Diffraction only in electromagnetic waves. ▼
✓ Answer: (c)I and II. Statement III is wrong — diffraction occurs in all waves including longitudinal mechanical waves.
66 Correct statements about Polaroid: I. Consists of long chain molecules aligned in a particular direction. II. Electric vectors along aligned molecules get absorbed. III. Unpolarised light incident on polaroid gets linearly polarised. ▼
✓ Answer: (d)All three statements are correct about how a Polaroid works.

Diagram Type Questions

Questions 70 – 78

70 Two coherent point sources S₁ and S₂ are separated by small distance 'd'. The fringes on the screen will be ▼
✓ Answer: (d)For a screen perpendicular to the line joining the two sources, the fringes are concentric circles.
71 Two coherent sources S₁ & S₂. AB is an irregular wire at far distance. λ/d = 10⁻³ and ∠BOA = 0.12°. How many bright spots on the wire including A and B? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)n = 0.12° × 2π / (360 × 10⁻³) ≈ 2.09. So 3 bright spots.
72 YDSE: S₁S₂ = 0.5 mm, D = 50 cm. Hole 1.0 mm from centre. White light 400–700 nm. Which wavelength through the hole has strong intensity? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)λ = xd/nD = (1×10⁻³ × 0.5×10⁻³)/(n × 50×10⁻²) = 1000/n nm. For n=2, λ = 500 nm ✓.
73 Parallel beam of white light on slits separated by d, with one slit at 2d/3 offset from centre. Distance of nearest white spot from O = d/A. Find A. ▼
✓ Answer: (c)y = 2d/3 − d/2 = d/6 → A = 6.
74 Two independent sodium lamps illuminate two slits S₁ and S₂. The screen will have ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Independent sources lack constant phase difference → incoherent → no interference, just uniform illumination.
75 In YDSE, monochromatic source is shifted to S' at angle φ above SQ. Position of central bright fringe will be ▼
✓ Answer: (c)When source shifts by +φ, central fringe shifts to −φ (S', midpoint Q, and central fringe are collinear).
76 Single-slit diffraction with electric bulb (white light) as source. The pattern will be ▼
✓ Answer: (c)Higher wavelength (red) → wider fringes; lower wavelength (blue) → narrower fringes.
77 Fraunhofer diffraction — first minimum in the shown direction. Path difference between rays 1 and 3 is ▼
✓ Answer: (c)For first minimum: Δx = mλ/2, m = 1 → Δx = λ/2. Ray 1 and ray 3 span half the slit.
78 Plane transmission grating. Diffracted images A, B, C = 1st, 2nd, 3rd order. Source replaced by shorter wavelength. The images shift ▼
✓ Answer: (c)d sin θ = nλ. Shorter λ → smaller θ → A, B, C all shift towards O (direct image stays fixed).

ReasonIn YDSE, if slit widths are unequal, the minima will be completely dark.
▼
✓ Answer: (d)Assertion is wrong — introducing a film of higher μ in front of upper slit increases optical path for upper slit → central fringe shifts upward. Reason is also wrong (unequal widths → minima not completely dark).

Critical Thinking Type Questions

Questions 91 – 141

91 On a hot summer night, refractive index of air is smallest near ground and increases with height. A horizontal light beam will ▼
✓ Answer: (b)Near ground μ is smallest (higher speed), upper portion of wavefront travels slower → wavefront tilts → beam bends upward (mirage phenomenon).
92 Two coherent sources of amplitude A, wavelength λ. Central maximum intensity = I₀. If sources were incoherent, intensity at same point? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Coherent: I₀ = 4I. Incoherent: I_R = I + I = 2I = I₀/2.
93 Two light waves (coherent, A₁ = A₂ = 1 cm, phase difference = 3π rad) superimpose. Resultant amplitude? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)A = √(1² + 1² + 2·1·1·cos 3π) = √(2 + 2·(−1)) = 0. Destructive interference.
94 Two coherent sources of intensity ratio 1:4 produce an interference pattern. The fringe visibility will be ▼
✓ Answer: (b)V = 2√(I₁I₂)/(I₁+I₂) = 2√(k·4k)/(k+4k) = 4k/5k = 0.8.
95 Two beams I₁ and I₂ interfere. I_max/I_min = 25/9. Then I₁/I₂ = ▼
✓ Answer: (d)(a₁+a₂)²/(a₁−a₂)² = 25/9 → (a₁+a₂)/(a₁−a₂) = 5/3 → 3a₁+3a₂ = 5a₁−5a₂ → 8a₂ = 2a₁ → a₁/a₂ = 4 → I₁/I₂ = 16.
96 YDSE with sodium lamp (λ = 589 nm), slits 0.589 mm apart. Half angular width of central maximum? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)sin θ = λ/d = 589×10⁻⁹ / 0.589×10⁻³ = 10⁻³ → θ = sin⁻¹(0.001).
97 Ratio of intensities at P and Q where phase difference is (a) 0° and (b) π/2 respectively? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)I ∝ 1 + cos φ. At φ=0: I_P ∝ 1+1=2. At φ=π/2: I_Q ∝ 1+0=1. Ratio = 2:1.
98 Laser λ₁ = 540 nm, fringe separation 9.00 mm. Second light gives 8.1 mm fringe separation. Wavelength of second light? ▼
✓ Answer: (b)β ∝ λ → λ₂ = λ₁ × β₂/β₁ = 540 × 8.1/9.0 = 486 nm.
99 YDSE: S above B (horizontal slits A and B). Optical path SB increased by transparent material. Fringe pattern moves ▼
✓ Answer: (b)Optical path through lower slit B is increased → central fringe shifts toward lower slit → downward.
100 In YDSE, let β = fringe width, I₀ = central intensity. At distance x from centre, intensity is ▼
✓ Answer: (c)Phase = 2π·(xd/D)/λ = 2πx/β (since β = λD/d). I = I₀cos²(φ/2) = I₀cos²(πx/β).
101 YDSE: slits 2 mm apart, D = 2 m, λ₁ = 12000 Å, λ₂ = 10000 Å. Minimum distance from centre where bright fringes coincide? ▼
✓ Answer: (a)n₁λ₁ = n₂λ₂ → n × 12000 = (n+1) × 10000 → n = 5. y = 5 × 12000×10⁻¹⁰ × 2 / (2×10⁻³) = 6 mm.
102 Maximum number of interference maxima when slit separation = 2λ? ▼
✓ Answer: (b)d sin θ = nλ; d = 2λ → sin θ = n/2. Valid n: 0, ±1, ±2 → 5 maxima.
103 Intensity at a point with path difference λ/6 is I. If I₀ = maximum intensity, then I/I₀ = ? ▼
✓ Answer: (a)Phase = 2π × (λ/6)/λ = π/3. I = I₀cos²(π/6) = I₀ × (√3/2)² = 3I₀/4 → I/I₀ = 3/4.
104 YDSE: amplitude from one slit = 2 × other. Let Im = max intensity. Resultant I at phase φ is ▼
✓ Answer: (d)a₁ = a, a₂ = 2a. Im = 9a². Ir = a² + 4a² + 4a² cosφ = Im/9 [5 + 4cosφ] = Im/9 [1 + 8cos²(φ/2)].
105 Monochromatic light of λ₁ = 400 nm and λ₂ = 560 nm, slit separation 0.1 mm, screen 1 m away. Distance between areas of total darkness? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)(2n+1)λ₁/2 = (2m+1)λ₂/2 → ratio (2n+1)/(2m+1) = 560/400 = 7/5. Solving: n=3,m=2 and n=10,m=7. Δs = Dλ₁/d × [(2n₂+1)−(2n₁+1)]/2 = 28 mm.
106 YDSE: intensity at a point = ¼ of maximum. Angular position of this point? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)I = Imax/4 → cos²(φ/2) = 1/4 → φ/2 = π/3 → φ = 2π/3. Path diff = λ/3 = d sinθ → θ = sin⁻¹(λ/3d).
107 In YDSE, path difference (in terms of n) at half peak intensity points? ▼
✓ Answer: (b)I = I₀/2 → cos²(φ/2) = 1/2 → φ/2 = π/4(2n−1) → path diff = (2n−1)λ/4.
108 λ = 6.5×10⁻⁷ m, slits 1 mm apart, screen 1 m away. Distance between 3rd dark fringe and 5th bright fringe? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)x₅(bright) − x₃(dark) = 5β − 2.5β = 2.5β = 2.5 × 0.65 mm = 1.625 mm.
109 YDSE: slit separation d/3, screen distance 3D. Fringes in 1/3 m on screen with wavelength 3λ? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)β' = 3λ·3D/(d/3) = 27λD/d. Fringes in 1/3 m = (1/3)/β' = d/(81Dλ) → number × β' = 81Dλ/d.
110 YDSE: source separation 0.1 mm, D = 20 cm, λ = 5460 Å. Angular position of first dark fringe? ▼
✓ Answer: (b)x₁ = λD/(2d). Angular position ≈ x₁/D = λ/(2d) = 5460×10⁻¹⁰/(2×10⁻⁴) ≈ 0.16°.
111 Fringe separation in a double-slit = x. Whole arrangement dipped in water (μ = 4/3). New fringe separation? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)β' = β/μ = x/(4/3) = 0.75x.
112 YDSE: 10 fringes visible with 4000 Å. With 5000 Å light, number of fringes in same field of view? ▼
✓ Answer: (a)n ∝ 1/λ. n' = 10 × (4000/5000) = 8.
113 YDSE fringe width = 0.133 cm. Setup immersed in water (μ = 1.33). New fringe width? ▼
✓ Answer: (b)β' = β/μ = 0.133/1.33 = 0.1 cm.
114 λ = 600 nm, single slit 1 mm wide, screen 2 m away. Distance between first dark fringes on either side of central bright fringe? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Width = 2λD/a = 2 × 600×10⁻⁹ × 2 / 10⁻³ = 2.4×10⁻³ m = 2.4 mm.
115 YDSE: intensity at path difference λ is K. Intensity at path difference λ/4? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)Path λ → φ = 2π → I = 4I₀ = K. Path λ/4 → φ = π/2 → I' = 4I₀cos²(π/4) = 2I₀ = K/2.
116 Double slit: d = 1 mm, D = 1 m, λ = 500 nm. Width of each slit for 10 maxima of double slit within central maximum of single slit? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)10β = 2λD/a → a = 2d/(10) = d/5 = 0.2 mm.
117 Single slit of width 'a' = wavelength λ, screen at D. Width of central maximum? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Width = 2λD/a.
118 λ = 5000 Å, slit width = 2.5×10⁻⁴ cm. Angular position of second minimum from central maximum? ▼
✓ Answer: (b)For 2nd minimum: a sinθ = 2λ → sin θ = 2λ/a = 2×5000×10⁻¹⁰/(2.5×10⁻⁶) = 2/5 → θ = sin⁻¹(2/5).
119 YDSE: intensity at path difference λ is I. Intensity at path difference λ/4? ▼
✓ Answer: (b)Similar to Q115: path λ → I = 4I₀. Path λ/4 → I' = 2I₀ = I/2.
120 YDSE: glass plate μ = 1.5 shifts fringes by x. Another plate of same thickness shifts by (3/2)x. Refractive index of second plate? ▼
✓ Answer: (a)Shift ∝ (μ − 1). (μ₂ − 1)/(0.5) = 3/2 → μ₂ − 1 = 0.75 → μ₂ = 1.75.
121 Two-slit experiment: screen moved 5×10⁻² m towards slits, fringe width changes by 10⁻³ m. Slit separation 0.03 mm. Wavelength of light? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Δβ = λ·ΔD/d → λ = Δβ·d/ΔD = 10⁻³ × 0.03×10⁻³ / (5×10⁻²) = 6×10⁻⁷ m = 6000 Å.
122 Fresnel biprism: mica sheet (μ = 1.5, t = 6×10⁻⁶ m) shifts central fringe by 5 fringe widths. Wavelength of light? ▼
✓ Answer: (a)λ = (μ−1)t/n = (0.5 × 6×10⁻⁶)/5 = 6×10⁻⁷ m = 6000 Å.
123 λ = 600 nm, single slit 1 mm wide, screen 2 m. Distance between first dark fringes on either side? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Same as Q114. Width = 2λD/a = 2 × 600×10⁻⁹ × 2 / 10⁻³ = 2.4 mm.
124 Slit width 0.012 mm. First bright line at 5.2° (sin 5.2° = 0.0906). Wavelength of incident light? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)First bright: a sinθ = (2n+1)λ/2 (n=1) → λ = 2a sinθ/3 = 2×1.2×10⁻⁵×0.0906/3 = 7248 Å.
125 λ = 6328 Å, slit width 0.2 mm, screen 9 m away. Angular width of central maximum? ▼
✓ Answer: (a)2θ = 2λ/a = 2×6328×10⁻¹⁰/(2×10⁻⁴) = 6.328×10⁻³ rad = 0.36°.
126 λ = 6250 Å, slit width 2×10⁻² cm, screen 50 cm. Width of principal maximum? ▼
✓ Answer: (a)Width = 2λD/a = 2×6250×10⁻¹⁰×0.5/(2×10⁻⁴) = 3.125×10⁻³ m = 312.5×10⁻³ cm.
127 Slit illuminated with red light λ = 6500 Å. First minimum at θ = 30°. Value of slit width 'a'? ▼
✓ Answer: (b)a sinθ = λ → a = λ/sin30° = 6500×10⁻¹⁰/0.5 = 1.3×10⁻⁶ m = 1.3 micron.
128 Parallel beam on corrected convex lens. The image will be ▼
✓ Answer: (b)Even an aberration-free lens cannot focus to a perfect point; diffraction limits it to a finite spot (Airy disc).
129 Fraunhofer diffraction, focal length = 1 m, slit width 0.3 mm. Third minimum at 5 mm from central maximum. Wavelength? ▼
✓ Answer: (a)sinθ ≈ x/f = 5/1000. a sinθ = 3λ → λ = 0.3×10⁻³ × 5×10⁻³/(3×1) = 5×10⁻⁷ m = 5000 Å.
130 White light single-slit. 3rd secondary maximum coincides with 2nd secondary maximum of red (6500 Å). Wavelength of other light? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)(2×3+1)λ = (2×2+1)×6500 → 7λ = 5×6500 → λ = 4642.8 Å.
131 Angle of incidence for totally polarised reflected light from air to glass (index n)? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)Brewster's angle: tan iB = n → iB = tan⁻¹(n).
132 Unpolarised light I₀ on a polarizing sheet. Intensity NOT transmitted? ▼
✓ Answer: (b)Polaroid transmits I₀/2; the other I₀/2 is absorbed/untransmitted.
133 Unpolarised I₀ through polaroid A, then B at 45° to A. Intensity of emergent light? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)After A: I₀/2. After B (Malus): (I₀/2)cos²45° = I₀/4.
134 Light at Brewster's angle φ on glass (μ). Angle between reflected and refracted rays? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)By definition of Brewster's angle: reflected and refracted rays are perpendicular (90°).
135 Unpolarised 32 W/m² through 3 polarizers (last crossed with first). Emerging = 3 W/m². Intensity transmitted by first polarizer? ▼
✓ Answer: (b)First polarizer transmits I₀/2 = 32/2 = 16 W/m².
136 Monochromatic light on dielectric (μ = 1/√3). Reflected beam is completely polarised. Angle of incidence? ▼
✓ Answer: (a)tan iB = μ = 1/√3 → iB = 30°.
137 Two Nicols oriented with principal planes at 60°. Percentage of unpolarised light passing through the system? ▼
✓ Answer: (c)After Nicol 1: 50%. After Nicol 2 (Malus, 60°): 50 × cos²60° = 50 × 1/4 = 12.5%.
138 Beam through tourmaline A, then B (B parallel to A), emergent = I₀. B rotated 45°. Emergent intensity? ▼
✓ Answer: (a)Malus: I' = I₀ cos²45° = I₀/2.
139 Polarising angle for green light in glass = 54.74° (tan 54.74° = 1.414). Angle of minimum deviation for equilateral prism of same glass? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)μ = √2. A = 60°. μ = sin((A+δm)/2)/sin(A/2). √2 = sin((60+δm)/2)/sin30° → (60+δm)/2 = 45° → δm = 30°.
140 Angle of incidence 60° → reflected ray completely polarised. Velocity of light in glass? ▼
✓ Answer: (b)tan60° = √3 = μ → v_g = c/√3 = 3×10⁸/√3 = √3 × 10⁸ m/s.
141 Two plane polarised beams A and B (mutually ⊥). From position where A is max (B = 0), rotating polaroid 30° makes them equally bright. Ratio IA/IB? ▼
✓ Answer: (d)I_A' = IA cos²30° = 3IA/4. I_B' = IB cos²60° = IB/4. Setting equal: 3IA = IB → IA/IB = 1/3.

Key Formulae & Hints

Quick reference for all important formulae from this chapter

Interference (YDSE)

Fringe width: β = λD/d
Constructive: Δ = nλ
Destructive: Δ = (n+½)λ
Intensity: I = I₀ cos²(πx/β)
I_max: (√I₁ + √I₂)²
I_min: (√I₁ − √I₂)²
Visibility: V = (Imax−Imin)/(Imax+Imin)

Single-Slit Diffraction

Minima: a sinθ = nλ
Central max width: 2λD/a
Angular width: 2λ/a
Resolving power: Δθ = 0.61λ/a

Polarisation

Brewster's law: tan iB = μ
Malus' law: I = I₀ cos²θ
Unpolarised → polaroid: I = I₀/2

Thin Film & General

Film shift: λ = (μ−1)t/n
In medium: β' = β/μ
Huygens: secondary wavelets, forward only
Coherence: same λ, constant Δφ
